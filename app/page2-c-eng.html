"Minsk's hippies" is a tour into the hairy and liberated 60-ties and 70-ties. Minsk's youth listened to the new kind of music by the western radio-waves (that were muffeled by KGB). They were dreaming to play like "Beatles" and "Led Zeppelin". Of course, they didn't manage so... That was a time when Minsk's bands appeared, unique and distinctive ones, mixing modern rock rymthes with belarusian traditional music... This is a travelling around the places of glory and drama of minsk's rock'n'roll. Put on your old shoes and jeans, flower children are coming back in the city!

Points: the stadium, where the football match between hippies & metalheads happened (1986), the place of "Mroja" ("N.R.M.") band's first open-air performing, Philharmonic, KGB's muffler for foreign radiowaves, Young Communist League's rock-cafe, hippie Slava Maksakaŭ assassination's place, legendary Minsk hippies' meeting points: Broadway, cafe "Biarozka"'s stairs, Green entrance, Scrapyard, vulica Kastryčnickaja (graffiti), "Ǔ" gallery of contemporary arts (former recycling bottle center #1), soviet rock-star Viktor Tsoy's memorial wall, if possible: Polytechnic and Radio technical institutes (Minsk big-beat-bands' cradles) etc.
