And if Napoleon had a bike?.. We propose to our brave cyclists to dive into the atmosphere of the events of the cold November of 1812 and to cycle in the footsteps of famous battles on the Biarezina river. This is a cycling story about broken hopes and armies, heated impudence, soldiers’ wits and generals’ cowardice, incredible suffering and doomed heroism, about blood and water states of matter and about Belarusian swamps.

Additional costs:
  • escort vehicle or local trains tickets
  • museum entrance fee and museum tour guide service (at option)
  • bicycle rental (if needed)

Sites:

  • Barysaŭ: constructions of praspiekt Revaliucyji of the XIX-XX cent., Shukhov’s tower (1927), house-museum of Ivan Kolodeev, defensive constructions of 1812, Christ's Resurrection cathedral (1874), shopping arcade (XIX cent.), site of the castle, Roman catholic church (1807), synagogues (XX cent.);
  • Starabarysaŭ: site of ancient town, manor house (XIX cent.);
  • Studzionka and the Brylieŭskaje field: memorials of the battles of the 1812 on the Biarezina river;
