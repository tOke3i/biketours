These parkways and glades are hidden on the very edge of the city. This is a former estate of the Prušynskis, then a residence of the count Liubanski, famous by its splendid balls (dances), the place of Jadviha Kienievič’s (count Liubanski's wife) mysterious death, the beloved walking ways of the Belarusian writer Vincent Dunin-Marcinkievič, well-known artist Józef Peszka's place of painting, NKVD's headquarter for training saboteurs for sabotages in Eastern Poland, a place of Stalin's massacres and then, nazi's headquarter meetings point, the base for science botanic researches under ruling of the world-known soviet geneticist Nikolai Vavilov... There are so many stratums for the space, chronometers are going off-scale, you should be careful not to be lost in the time! The shadows of the dead are trailing in parallel with those who are alive, wandering by the lost paths, hiding in the depth of the park, flickering among the old trees... We are waiting for you, our brave cycling researchers. We guarantee your coming back into XXI cent.

Sites: old linden alley (XVIII cent.), centuries old oaks, exotic trees, geneticist Nikolai Vavilov's gardens, count Liubanski's homestead (XIX cent.), old water mills and brewery, World War II monument, Čorny jar (Stalin's massacres place), the Prušynskis' Crypt Chapel (XVIII cent.) etc. After the tour, the visit of the museum “The estate of Lošyca” is possible (at a cost).
