Who told you that Minsk is on its original place? And what sort of ramparts do overlook not far away from the modern metropolis beside a river with the speaking name Mienka? We're going to touch the secret which is inside of the impressive earth fortress, probably called Miensk in the past... Before this, by green country path, we'll reach a famous open air museum near Aziarco village. There you'll see the most interesting and distinctive examples of Belarusian traditional architecture. Authentic only! There are all gradations of natural wood and stone, all diversity of wooden constructions that could be made by the Belarusians including houses with farms, a tavern, a school, churches, mills etc. All these riches of culture are situated amid green carpets which cover the land since May annually. At the end, after all ups and downs of Minsk district, we'll make a cycling promenade along a border of the artificial lake of Pcič, looking for the best place at the beach or in a local cafe!...

Additional costs:

  • bicycle rental (if needed)

Sites: Belarusian state museum of folk architecture and rural lifestyle (1,5 hour cycling tour), site of an ancient settlement of the X cent. near Mienka river, artificial lake of Pcič.
