This is a tour into the hairy and liberated 1960-70s. Minsk's youth listened to the new kind of music broadcasted by the western radio-waves (that were muffled by KGB). They were dreaming to play like "Beatles" and "Led Zeppelin". Of course, they didn't manage... That was a time when Minsk's bands appeared, unique and distinctive ones, mixing modern rock rhythms with the Belarusian folk music... This is a tour around the sites of glory and drama, famous gigs and funny stories of Minsk’s hippies. Put on your old shoes and jeans, flower children are coming back to the city!

Sites: the stadium, where the football match between hippies & metalheads happened (1986), the place of "Mroja" ("N.R.M.") band's first open-air performing, Philharmonic, KGB's jam station for muffling foreign radio waves, Young Communist League's rock-cafe, assassination place of Slava Maksakaŭ hippie, former recycling bottle center #1, Minsk hippies' meeting points: Broadway, cafe "Biarozka"'s stairs, Green entrance, Scrapyard, Polytechnic and Radio technical institutes (Minsk big-beat-bands' cradles) etc. Bonus track for fast cyclists: vulica Kastryčnickaja (street of murals), Soviet rockstar Viktor Tsoy's memorial wall.
